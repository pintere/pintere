My story.

IN A NUTSHELL WHO I AM

I'm a curious person who loves exploring new things - from travel and culinary adventures to simply discovering a new route home from the office. My curiosity extends to my relationships too; I'm happiest when I'm around people who share their thoughts, stories, or feelings with me. This is what I'm so passionate about even in my work.

Moving to a new country and adapting to a different language - first studying in Belgium, then relocating to Prague for love and for a new career opportunity - has been an exciting challenge that taught me a lot about resilience and patience.

I recently became a swimming instructor, which has become another hobby I enjoy: teaching children and adults to swim to help them feel comfortable in the water.

In my free time, besides swimming, I also enjoy yoga, hiking, and experimenting with crafts and painting. Growing up in Hungary and navigating a highly political environment sparked my interest in politics, which continues to be a key focus of mine.

Expertise

As a sociologist, I'm specialized in both quantitative and qualitative research, including digital insights / social listening.
My researcher background is more qualitative, I led focus groups and interviews for academic and commercial purposes.
One of my most meaningful projects was a narrative (lifeway) interview study focusing on the Second World War and the Shoah. Our research team conducted in-depth interviews with World War II survivors to explore how shared historical events are narrated from different perspectives, and to understand the internal psychological and external social processes that shape personal narratives.
I've spent years digging into online conversations - analysing comments, stories, and debates - to understand what really drives people. I have explored complex topics such as the meaning of motherhood for a telecommunications company, personal loan-related narratives for a financial institution, cooking-related conversations for a global FMCG company, or the different perceptions of trust across car brand for an international automotive brand - translating online user stories and narratives into actionable insights. My work focused on uncovering what truly motivates people - their needs, pain points, emotions, and underlying drivers - by deeply analysing user comments and short stories. I also developped online data-based personas , defining their characteristics, interests, and behavioural patterns to support brand strategy, communication, and innovation.
Having started my career in a small agency where constant innovation of the service offering was essential, I highly value research tools and solutions that bring fresh perspectives and present insights in new, more meaningful ways.
I am a member of Ipsos Synthesio's Client Strategic Board , a senior peer network of social and search intelligence leaders across industries. The board focuses on shaping the future of social and search intelligence by sharing best practices, evaluating emerging use cases, and piloting new capabilities.
In my current position I established a community of local insight leaders to embed digital listening into our ways of working across the organization. I designed and delivered training sessions for both core and lighter users, covering how to use our social listening tool, the types of questions it can answer, and practical applications. Within this community, members have shared use cases, discussed challenges, and collaboratively developed best practices to drive more effective and consistent insight generation across markets using digital data.
