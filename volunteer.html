VOLUNTEERING

it has a main role in my life

Since I was in high school, I have always done some volunteer jobs:

SZIGET FESTIVAL

I was working on the Sziget Festival for 7 years as an artist attendant (we called it “babysitter” because sometimes it was "babysitting"... artists can just disappear or get wander and forget the show): I was helping street theatre performers from all over the world to make their shows as smooth as possible. It needed my organisational and problem-solving skills when e.g. an Australian theatrical group needed a pair of sausages 5 minutes before their show or when a German company’s 100-year-old bicycle broke and I managed to replace it during the day (searching in all of the bike service stores in the city).

Educational mentoring

Nowadays I am a mentor for a sociology BA student. He has some learning difficulties, we have been studying and writing homework together. I have been there for him to consult papers for uni, to discuss his thesis or to prepare for admission to the master's program.

Mentoring refugees

In 2020, during the quarantine all of a sudden, I had a lot of free time, so I decided to find some other opportunity to spend it more helpful. Artemisszió Foundation organized a course to train mentors to help immigrants and refugees in their daily lives, provide useful information and promote their social integration. So I joined them on a weekend in November and since then I am mentoring an Afghan mother (and her family) who lives here for a year. We meet regularly and I try to help them learning Hungarian and socializing in the Budapest life. Besides the language difficulties (their Hungarian is rudimentary and they don’t speak other than farsi) I can talk mostly with the kids who soak up the language like a sponge. With this program I also became a member of MIRA Intercultural Community, that organizes meetups and other community bulding events and courses.

Budapest100

Budapest100 is a civil urban weekend-festival, where (about) 100-year-old houses all around Budapest open to everyone. "Budapest100 is a spring weekend celebration of the people and buildings around us." I have been participating as a visitor in this event for several years. I always waited with great excitement to see open houses and learn about the microhistory of the people living there. This year I joined them as an organizer volunteer: I asked residents is they want their house to participate i the festival and started to organize events. Even the house where I live is celebrating this year, so I cannot be more excited.
